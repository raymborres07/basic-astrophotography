The "Diskarte" Cheat Sheet

1. Weather & Sky

  • Cloud Cover: Check Clear Outside or Windy. Cloud cover is a big NO-NO.
  • Moon Phase: Shoot during New Moon or after moonset for maximum darkness.
  • Bortle Scale: Know your location. Bortle 1-3 is the dream; 4-5 is okay with filters.

2. Gear & Etiquette

  • Red Light Only: Use a red headlamp. White light ruins your night vision and "photobombs" long exposures.
  • Camping Gear: If you're going far, bring a power bank, warm clothes, and coffee.
  • Stability: Use a beanbag or camera bag if you don't have a tripod.

3. The 500 Rule

500 ÷ Focal Length = Max Exposure (s)

For APS-C sensors, use 300 instead of 500.

4. Post-Processing Workflow

  • White Balance: Lower Temperature (Kelvin) to ~3200K to neutralize orange light pollution.
  • Curves: Use an "S-Curve" to boost contrast without clipping stars.
  • Noise Reduction: Don't overdo it! Preserve the fine star details.
