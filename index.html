1. Hardware Reality & Selection

Phones (The Zero-Budget Setup)

Phone sensors are tiny and fixed-aperture (f/1.7-2.2). The Fix: Shoot in RAW and stack multiple images to average out noise.

DSLRs & Mirrorless (The Enthusiast)

Large sensors drink in light at ISO 3200-6400. The Bottleneck: Cold weather kills batteries; may need external remotes.

Hardware Hacks

  • Zero-Vibration: Use wired earphone volume buttons as a remote shutter.
  • Manual Focus: Zoom in 10x on a star; adjust until it's the smallest dot possible.
  • The Dew Hack: Wrap a chemical hand warmer (kairo) around the lens.

2. Planning & Weather

The Golden Rules

  • Cloud Cover: Use Clear Outside. Cloud cover is a big NO-NO.
  • PAGASA: Cross-reference with localized satellite imagery in PH.
  • Bortle Scale: Use LightPollutionMap.info to find Bortle 4 or lower.
  • Moon Phase: Shoot when illumination is < 20% or after moonset.

Planning Apps

  • Stellarium / Star Walk 2: Virtual sky to see where the Milky Way rises.
  • Red Light Rule: Use red headlamps only to protect night vision.
  • Camping: Bring power banks and warm clothes for long sessions.

3. Capture & Automation

The 500 Rule Equation

500 ÷ FL = Max Exposure (s)

*For APS-C sensors (Sony/Canon/Nikon entry), use 300 instead of 500.

Free RAW Camera Apps

  • Lightroom Mobile: Pro camera shoots true DNG RAW files.
  • Open Camera: Free, open-source, and allows manual infinity focus.
  • Intervalometer (Android): Auto-clicker for capturing 100+ stacking frames.

4. Post-Processing Stack

Stacking Software (Noise Removal)

  • Sequator (Windows): The easiest stacking tool for beginners.
  • DeepSkyStacker (DSS): The industry standard for deep-sky work.

Editing & Color Pop

  • Kill the Orange: Drop White Balance to ~3200K to dim city haze.
  • HSL Panel: Drop Orange Luminance to selectively dim light pollution.
  • Vibrance: Boost Vibrance (not Saturation) to protect star colors.
  • Darktable: Free, professional open-source alternative to Lightroom.
